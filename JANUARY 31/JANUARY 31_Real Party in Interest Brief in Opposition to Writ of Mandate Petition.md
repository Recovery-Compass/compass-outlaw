The Real Party in Interest (Heidi Jones Blanchard) argues that the Petitioner's (Eric Brakebill Jones's) Code of Civil Procedure (CCP) Section 170.6 challenge to Hon. Elisabeth K. Mineta was properly denied for two main reasons:

1. **Challenge Exhausted:** The statute strictly limits each party to a **single peremptory challenge** in any one action. Mr. Jones had already exercised his one statutory challenge by filing a written motion under CCP Section 170.6 on January 5, 2026, directed at **Honorable Julie R. Culver**.  
2. **Untimeliness:** Even if his challenge was not already exhausted, his oral motion on January 7, 2026, was **per se untimely** because it was made *during the hearing itself*, after he had appeared and participated before Judge Mineta. The right to a challenge must be exercised at the earliest practicable opportunity, and the statute does not authorize oral, mid-hearing challenges.

Argument Against the Request for a Stay

The Real Party in Interest argues that the Petitioner's request for an immediate stay is **not appropriate or necessary** for the following reasons:

* The request is based on **conclusory allegations of asset dissipation** which are unrelated to the judicial disqualification issue.  
* A stay is an extraordinary remedy that requires a **clear, specific, and imminent showing of irreparable harm**, which the Petitioner has not provided with "competent evidence."  
* The Executor (Heidi Jones Blanchard) was **required to provide a bond**, and the Probate Court is fully capable of overseeing estate assets and addressing any proper accounting through normal, statutory procedures.  
* Granting a stay based on unverified allegations would **unfairly prejudice the administration of the Estate** and the interests of all beneficiaries.
